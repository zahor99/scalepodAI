# Design-by-Design Competitive Analysis
## Our 9 Patterns vs. Google Shopping Competition

**Date:** March 8, 2026
**Source data:** Airtable Shopify Products (40 SKUs), Google Shopping SERPs (4 niches), competitive review findings

---

## Executive Summary

After visually comparing all 9 of our patterns against top-selling Google Shopping competitors in each niche, here are the headline findings:

**What we're doing RIGHT:**
- Dark backgrounds are spot-on for Dark Floral, Whimsigoth, and Celestial niches
- Most motif density levels match market standards (55-80% range)
- Pattern quality and print resolution are competitive with mid-tier sellers ($40-$70 range)

**What needs fixing:**
- **Pricing is too low across the board.** Our $34.99-$53.99 sale prices undercut the market but destroy margins after Google Shopping ads. Competitors sell at $40-$89+ for comparable AOP dresses.
- **Celestial niche is unprofitable** — $0.48 CPC makes ALL Celestial products money-losers after ads. Either raise prices significantly or move Celestial to Etsy-only.
- **Boho Floral patterns miss the mark** — competitors use light/cream backgrounds with earth tones. Our BF-01 is close but BF-03 (sage green bg) is unusual for the niche.
- **Slim Spaghetti dresses lose money in EVERY niche** — $34.99 sale price can't absorb ad costs. Drop this dress type from Google Shopping entirely.

**Bottom-line recommendations:**
1. Raise prices $5-$15 across all dresses (details per pattern below)
2. Kill Celestial on Google Shopping — move to Etsy organic only
3. Redesign BF-03 with a cream/ivory background
4. Drop Slim Spaghetti dress from paid channel
5. DF-05 is your strongest cross-niche asset — it already works for Art Nouveau and Dark Cottagecore

## Answering Your Specific Questions

### "Should I make larger, less dense motifs — or the other way around?"

**Go LARGER on motifs, especially for:**
- **Boho Floral (BF-01, BF-03):** Competitors use oversized wildflowers. Our ditsy/medium patterns look budget compared to Baltic Born and Nordstrom's large-scale prints. Increase motif size 30-50%.
- **Dark Floral (DF-02):** The hero roses should be bigger. DF-04 already has the right large scale and is our strongest seller for this reason.

**Keep density where it is for:**
- **Whimsigoth (WG-04, WG-05):** 65-70% density is perfect for the niche. Competitors don't go sparse.
- **Celestial (CL-04, CL-05):** 55-60% is appropriate — celestial patterns have breathing room by design.

**Summary:** Larger motifs + maintain density = more premium look. The patterns that best match competitor standards (DF-04, WG-05) are the ones with larger motifs. The weakest performers (BF-01, BF-03) have the smallest motifs.

### "Am I using the right color combinations?"

**YES for:**
- **WG-05 (Celestial Witch):** Gold/purple/red on deep purple is textbook whimsigoth. Keep it.
- **DF-04 (Moonlit Dahlia):** Red/cream/pink on black is exactly what sells in dark floral. A touch more gold would be even better.
- **WG-04 (Raven & Roses):** Gold/green/red on black works. More purple would make it more "whimsigoth" and less "gothic."
- **CL-05 (Zodiac Medallion):** Gold/bronze on black is correct for celestial.

**NEEDS ADJUSTMENT for:**
- **DF-02 (Noir Rose & Butterfly):** Too much orange. Replace orange with blush/gold to match the dark floral standard.
- **DF-05 (Dark Garden Tapestry):** Teal is a bold choice. It works but is unusual — consider a gold/bronze variant.
- **BF-01 (Wildflower Meadow):** Too narrow a palette (just orange/pink/rust). Add sage green, teal, marigold.
- **BF-03 (Garden Party):** Purple roses are wrong for boho. Replace with rust/terracotta/marigold. And switch the sage green background to cream.
- **CL-04 (Cosmic Garden):** Pink/mauve is unusual for celestial. Replace with pure silver/gold metallics.
